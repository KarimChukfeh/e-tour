⏱️ Time Bank System (Chess Clock)

Fischer Increment Time Bank Mechanics: Initial Setup: Chess: 600 seconds (10 min) + 15s per move ConnectFour: 120 seconds (2 min) + 15s per move TicTacToe: 300 seconds (5 min) + 15s per move Example Game Flow (ConnectFour): T=0: Player1 bank: 120s | Player2 bank: 120s Player1 moves (3s elapsed) T=3: Player1 bank: 120 - 3 + 15 = 132s ↑ Player2 bank: 120s Player2 moves (5s elapsed) T=8: Player1 bank: 132s Player2 bank: 120 - 5 + 15 = 130s ↑ Time Bank Benefits: ✓ Fast players GAIN time (Fischer increment reward) ✓ Complex positions get more thinking time ✓ Prevents clock stalling strategies ✓ Fair timeout detection at any point

Why Fischer Increment? Traditional chess clocks penalize slower play. Fischer increment (invented by Bobby Fischer) adds time after each move, rewarding players who move quickly while still allowing adequate time for complex positions. This prevents the "time scramble" endgame where players make random moves to avoid timeout.
